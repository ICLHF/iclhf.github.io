We introduce In-Context Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (IC-RLHF) algorithm that learns human preferences in situ and combines them with physical constraints to accomplish the task.

Abstract

Equipped with Large Language Models (LLMs), human-centered robots are now capable of completing a wide range of tasks that were previously considered difficult or impossible. However, the completion of many tasks is not enough for cognitive robots, especially for service robots, which should learn human preferences and apply them to future scenarios. In this work, we propose a framework that combines physical constraints and human preferences, which requires robots to complete tasks while taking into account physical feasibility and user preferences. First, we designed a benchmark that contains everyday household activities whose assessment is often interspersed with specific preferences. Then we proposed In-Context Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF), a method of performing RLHF in the form of In-Context Learning, in which human feedback comes from direct instructions, and adjustments made intentionally or unintentionally in daily life. Extensive sets of experiments, testing the LLM's ability to generate task plans and trade-offs between physical constraints and preferences, have demonstrated the efficiency of our method.

A Brief Overview of IC-RLHF Workflow

IC-RLHF algorithm includes five steps:
  1. Make initial plan with user's instruction and summarized preference
  2. Three process for the preliminary planning of LLMs
  3. Use POG, an algorithm for efficient sequential manipulation planning on the scene graph, to refine the details of the planning
  4. Execute the plan and get physical feedback
  5. Reflect human preference and update the plan
